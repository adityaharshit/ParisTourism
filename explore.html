Explore the Romantic Allure of Paris

Discover the magic and romance that Paris has to offer

Paris History

Paris, known as the 'City of Light', has a rich history dating back to the Roman era. The city has been a center of art, culture, and revolution. Explore the historical landmarks that tell the story of Paris' past, from the Gothic architecture of Notre-Dame Cathedral to the grandeur of the Palace of Versailles.

Iconic Landmarks

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris, was built in 1889 for the World’s Fair. Standing at 330 meters, this iron structure offers panoramic views of the city. Designed by Gustave Eiffel, it’s visited by millions yearly, illuminated at night, and a must-see landmark.

Louvre Musuem

The Louvre Museum is the world’s largest art museum, housing over 38,000 works, including the famous Mona Lisa. Originally a royal palace, it features a vast collection spanning ancient civilizations to modern times. Its glass pyramid entrance is a modern architectural marvel, welcoming millions of visitors yearly.

Arc De Triumphe

The Arc de Triomphe is a monumental arch in Paris, commissioned by Napoleon in 1806 to honor his army's victories. Standing at the center of Place Charles de Gaulle, it features intricate carvings and names of soldiers. Visitors can climb to its top for breathtaking views of the city.

Romantic Activites

Paris offers a plethora of romantic activities for couples. Take a leisurely boat ride on the Seine River, enjoy a picnic at the Champ de Mars with the Eiffel Tower as your backdrop, or take a stroll through the charming streets of Montmartre. Each moment in Paris can be a cherished memory.

Romantic Activites

Paris Opera House

The Paris Opera House, or Palais Garnier, is a grand 19th-century building known for its opulent interiors and magnificent architecture. Designed by Charles Garnier, it’s home to classical ballet and opera performances. The stunning auditorium, chandeliers, and grand staircase make it a masterpiece of Baroque Revival style.

Montmarte Artists

Montmartre, a historic Parisian district, has long been a haven for artists like Picasso and Van Gogh. Known for its bohemian charm, it’s home to vibrant street art, portrait artists, and quaint cafes. Visitors flock to Place du Tertre, where artists create and sell their works in the open air.

Parisian Bistro

A Parisian bistro is a small, casual restaurant offering classic French dishes such as steak frites, croque monsieur, and coq au vin. Known for their cozy atmosphere and charming outdoor seating, bistros are integral to Paris’s culinary culture, often serving hearty meals with wine and friendly service.

Shopping and Fashion

Paris, a global fashion capital, is famed for luxury shopping, with iconic avenues like the Champs-Élysées and Rue Saint-Honoré hosting renowned brands like Chanel and Louis Vuitton. The city also boasts chic boutiques, concept stores, and vibrant markets, while Paris Fashion Week showcases cutting-edge trends and haute couture.

Shopping malls and streets

Champs Élysées & the Golden Triangle

Located between the Arc de Triomphe and the Franklin D. Roosevelt Metro Station, the commercial portion of Champs Élysées stretches for almost a mile through the swanky 8th arrondissement and is the most famous shopping avenue in Paris - a must-see destination for visitors from around the world

La Vallée Village

A sprawling discount designer fashion outlet mall just beyond the city outskirts. You won't find the most exclusive brands such as Hèrmes or Chanel here, but with 110+ shops filled with other sought-after French, European, and American designers - Givenchy, Isabel Marant, Carolina Herrera, Michael Kors, The Kooples.

Historical Covered Passages

Originally built to give shoppers a dry, safe, well-lighted place to walk, the passages are basically private lanes built between exisiting buildings and usually passing a cut-through between two major streets. They feature soaring glass roof, shops, bistros, and sometimes theaters along both sides, and in the most luxurious examples, sumptuous decor.
